# Intelligent Regenerative Braking System for an Electric Scooter

> Final-Year Engineering Project | Embedded Systems Engineering Internship at E-RIDER

## Overview

This project presents the design and development of an intelligent regenerative braking system for an electric scooter, aiming to recover kinetic energy during braking and reuse it to recharge the lithium battery. The system was developed as part of my Final-Year Engineering Project during a six-month internship at **E-RIDER**, combining embedded systems, power electronics, battery management, and mechanical integration.

The project demonstrates how embedded control can improve energy efficiency, extend battery life, and contribute to more sustainable electric mobility solutions.

---

## Project Objectives

- Recover electrical energy during vehicle braking.
- Improve the overall energy efficiency of the electric scooter.
- Reduce unnecessary battery discharge.
- Extend lithium battery lifespan through intelligent energy management.
- Design a reliable embedded control system using the ESP32.
- Integrate monitoring and protection functions for safe system operation.

## System Architecture

The embedded system continuously acquires data from multiple sensors, including battery voltage, current, temperature, and wheel speed. Based on these measurements, the ESP32 determines the appropriate operating mode and controls the regenerative braking process while monitoring battery protection and cooling requirements.

The regenerated electrical energy is processed through the power electronics stage before being redirected to recharge the lithium battery under safe operating conditions.
